# Insights

## The Core Finding

$37.6 million in estimated revenue at risk from unresolved customer friction across 100,000+ CFPB financial complaints. Three product categories account for 66% of that exposure. The complaint data was always there. The revenue translation was not.

---

## By the Numbers

**Overall:**
- 100,000 complaints analyzed across 9 product categories
- 85.3% of complaints closed without meaningful relief ("closed with explanation" or equivalent)
- 2.2% untimely response rate overall — higher in high-risk categories
- $37.6M total estimated revenue at risk

**By Category:**

| Category | Friction Score | Unresolved Rate | Revenue at Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Credit Reporting | 50.9 (Critical) | 91.9% | $13.4M |
| Debt Collection | 38.9 (Critical) | 88.1% | $7.1M |
| Credit Card | 33.6 (High) | 79.4% | $4.3M |
| Checking / Savings | 32.0 (High) | 73.8% | $4.7M |
| Mortgage | 32.4 (High) | 76.6% | $3.3M |

---

## Five Findings

**1. Volume is not the same as severity.**
Credit Reporting dominates complaint volume (43% of all complaints) but carries a lower per-complaint churn rate (18%) than Checking/Savings (35%). Volume and severity point in different directions. A prioritization model that optimizes only for volume will systematically underinvest in the highest-cost categories.

**2. "Closed with explanation" is not resolution.**
85.3% of complaints are closed without relief. The CFPB counts these as "responded to." The revenue model counts them as unresolved — because consumers who file a complaint, receive an explanation, and see no action taken behave like churners. The gap between regulatory compliance and customer retention is the gap this project is measuring.

**3. Untimely response doubles the problem.**
Complaints that receive a late response and no meaningful relief carry a 2x churn multiplier relative to timely-responded complaints. The categories with the highest untimely rates — Money Transfer (6.4%), Checking/Savings (4.5%), Payday/Personal Loan (4.0%) — are smaller in volume but represent outsized per-complaint revenue risk. Speed of response in high-urgency complaint categories is not a service metric. It is a retention metric.

**4. Checking and Savings is the highest per-complaint cost category.**
At 9% of total complaint volume and 12.4% of total revenue at risk, Checking and Savings produces more revenue loss per complaint than any other category. The combination of high churn rate (35%), above-average untimely response rate, and high-LTV customer segment makes this the highest-priority category for resolution investment — despite not being the loudest category by volume.

**5. Debt Collection carries compounding risk.**
The debt collection category has the highest consumer dispute rate of any category — meaning consumers are not just filing complaints, they are actively rejecting resolutions. This signals a population of highly dissatisfied customers who have already escalated once and been dismissed. Beyond direct churn, debt collection complaints carry ongoing regulatory attention from the CFPB. The financial exposure is not limited to LTV.

## The Measurement Gap (Applied to CX)

This project is an extension of the measurement gap thesis from the broader portfolio: institutions measure what is reportable, not what is consequential. In CX, "complaints responded to" is the reportable metric. "Revenue retained" is the consequential one. The friction score and revenue at risk model build the bridge between them.
